# Economics Analysis

## What A Converted Lead Is Worth To Credex

Credex sells discounted AI infrastructure credits to startups already spending heavily on AI tooling and APIs.

A reasonable assumption is:

* average converted customer spends between $3,000–$15,000 annually on AI tooling
* Credex captures margin through discounted infrastructure resale or brokerage

Assuming:

* average first-year customer value = $5,000
* Credex captures approximately 20% effective margin

A converted customer may realistically generate approximately:

```txt
$1,000 gross profit
```

per successful conversion.

This estimate intentionally assumes smaller startup customers rather than large enterprise contracts.

---

# Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

Because this product acts as both:

* a useful free tool
* and a lead-generation engine

distribution can remain relatively inexpensive if sharing and community engagement work effectively.

---

## Organic Founder Communities

Channels:

* Hacker News
* Reddit
* Indie Hackers
* startup Slack groups
* engineering Discord communities

Estimated CAC:

```txt
$10–$40 per qualified lead
```

primarily representing founder outreach time and content creation effort.

---

## Product Hunt Launch

A successful Product Hunt launch could temporarily reduce CAC because:

* discovery is community-driven
* users self-select into the target audience
* the product naturally encourages discussion around AI tooling costs

Estimated CAC:

```txt
Under $20 per qualified lead
```

during launch periods.

## Paid Acquisition (Future Scenario)

If paid acquisition were introduced later through:

* Google Ads

* startup SaaS targeting

CAC would likely increase significantly:

```txt
$75–$200+ per qualified lead
```

depending on keyword competition and conversion quality.

# Conversion Funnel Assumptions

The product becomes economically viable if a reasonable percentage of audit users become qualified infrastructure leads.

Example funnel:

```txt
1000 audit users
→ 180 lead captures
→ 30 Credex consultations
→ 6 paying customers
```

---

## Conversion Rates

### Audit → Lead Capture

```txt
18%
```

This is realistic because users receive value before the email request appears.

---

### Lead → Consultation

```txt
~16%
```

Only users discovering meaningful savings opportunities are likely to schedule consultations.

---

### Consultation → Credit Purchase

```txt
~20%
```

Reasonable for warm inbound startup leads already experiencing AI infrastructure cost pain.

| Lead captures               |        18% of audits |           180 |
| Credex consultations booked |         16% of leads |            30 |

| Gross profit per customer   |            Estimated |        $1,000 |
| Monthly gross profit        |           6 × $1,000 |        $6,000 |

# Break-Even Logic

If organic/community CAC averages:

```txt
$30 per qualified lead
```

Then:

```txt
180 leads × $30 CAC = $5,400 acquisition cost
```

If 6 customers convert:

```txt
6 customers × $1,000 gross profit = $6,000 gross profit
```

Under these assumptions, the model becomes roughly break-even or slightly profitable even at relatively small scale.

The economics improve substantially if:

* more high-savings audits are captured
* larger startups convert
* consultation conversion rates improve
* sharing reduces effective acquisition costs further

# What Needs To Be True To Reach $1M ARR In 18 Months

To approach:

```txt
$1M ARR
```

the product would likely require:

* strong viral sharing behavior
* repeated founder community exposure
* reliable organic acquisition loops
* high-quality AI cost optimization recommendations
* strong conversion among API-heavy startups

Example scale scenario:

```txt
10,000 audits/month
→ 1,800 captured leads
→ 300 consultations
→ 60 customers/month
```

If customer contracts average meaningful recurring infrastructure spend, this begins approaching sustainable seven-figure annual revenue scale.

---

# Biggest Economic Risk

The biggest economic risk is that many users may:

* complete audits
* enjoy the free recommendations
* but never require infrastructure-scale purchasing support

The product becomes significantly more valuable when:

* high-spend engineering teams adopt it
* API-heavy startups use it repeatedly
* audits consistently identify meaningful recurring overspend

This means distribution quality and audience targeting matter more than raw traffic volume.
